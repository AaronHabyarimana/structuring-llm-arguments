\chapter{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\section{What the Formal Layer Adds}
\label{sec:discussion:value}

The cross-topic results in Section~\ref{sec:evaluation:crosstopic} show that the
formal layer does uncover structure. Six of eight topics support several
internally consistent positions, and every framework is cyclic. At the same time
the value of the expensive preferred and stable enumeration over the cheap
grounded extension is uneven. On several topics the grounded extension already
decides most arguments, so preferred and stable add little, because the
extraction tends to produce nearly acyclic graphs outside the symmetrized
rebuttals. The clearest payoff appears on topics such as Death Penalty, Gun
Control, and Minimum Wage, where three distinct stable extensions emerge. An
honest reading is therefore that the formal layer is most useful where the
debate genuinely splits into competing coherent camps, and closer to redundant
where one side dominates.

\section{Threats to Validity}
\label{sec:discussion:threats}

Several threats qualify the results. The confidence score is a routing signal
and not a calibrated probability, so the thresholds are design choices rather
than learned values. In the LLM-generated setting the arguments, the attacks,
and the confidence scores all come from the same model, so there is no
independent quality signal, and that setting is treated as a qualitative check
rather than a second evaluation. The UKP samples are small, with a balanced
draw per topic, which limits how far the cross-topic numbers generalise. Finally,
all results stem from a single model at temperature zero, and server-side
execution does not guarantee perfect determinism.

\section{Design Trade-offs}
\label{sec:discussion:tradeoffs}

Three design decisions shape the frameworks and deserve explicit discussion. The
symmetrization of rebuttals is well motivated by the absence of a preference
ordering in Dung's abstract framework, but it is also the single source of every
cycle, which makes a mislabelled rebuttal costly. Dropping the attack type when
converting to binary attack facts is consistent with the set-based question Dung
semantics answer, yet it discards information that a richer formalism could use.
The confidence thresholds trade precision against recall, and the verification
step exists precisely to recover borderline cases without lowering the
acceptance threshold for everyone.
